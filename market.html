Competitive Landscape

What exists — and what’s missing

Several tools touch parts of the problem. None of them address the full live-delivery experience the way Pretive does.

Competitor What They Do What They Miss
Microsoft Copilot Slide creation, Speaker Coach ✕ No live screen enrichment, no participant agent, no fact-check
Canva Visual presentations, Canva Live ✕ No semantic flow tracking, no background agent
PromptSmart Voice-synced teleprompter ✕ Prompter only — no new content generation, no quiz, no participant interaction
Gamma Programmatic presentation creation ✕ No live delivery orchestration, no style learning
Otter Transcription, meeting agent ✕ No education-focused narration management, no participant side
Mentimeter / Slido Surveys and Q&A ✕ Interaction only — no narration management, no content enrichment

Why Pretive Wins

Five structural advantages

1

Big players focus elsewhere. Microsoft, Canva, and others concentrate on content creation and general meeting assistance — not live delivery.

2

Pretive owns the live execution layer. We are purpose-built for the moment of delivery — when the speaker is actually talking and participants are actively engaged.

3

Two-sided platform creates deeper loyalty. Both the speaker and the participant benefit, making the product stickier than any single-sided tool.

4

Data and style learning build a moat. Education data, personal style calibration, and habitual use create compounding switching costs over time.

5

Additive, not replacement. Pretive works on top of existing tools like Zoom, Google Meet, and PowerPoint — lowering adoption friction dramatically.
